Welcome

This page was created as part of a bachelor thesis. is to test the accuracy and robustness of various algorithms for causal inference. The simulator allows the user to input, or to generate randomly, complex causal structures. Each causal structure defines a so-called coincidence table, in which all permissible co-occurrences of variables are listed. It is assumed that coincidence tables are the empirical manifestation of complex causal structures. Our algorithms are then concerned with inferring the original causal structure from its coincidence table.

Causal Theory

Our approach is based on the regularity theoretic approach to causality developed in Baumgartner and Graßhoff (2004) and Baumgartner (2008). We have so far implemented the algorithm described in Baumgartner (2009).

References

  • Baumgartner, M. 2009. Uncovering deterministic causal structures: a Boolean approach. Synthese 170: 71-96.
